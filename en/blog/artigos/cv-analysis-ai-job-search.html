01 — CV Analysis

How to Analyse Your CV with AI (and What the Algorithm Actually Checks)

AI-powered CV analysis has become one of the most powerful tools in any modern job search. Instead of waiting weeks for recruiter feedback, you can understand in seconds exactly what is blocking your applications.

A proper resume analysis evaluates keyword alignment with job descriptions, the clarity of quantified achievements, section structure, and ATS (Applicant Tracking System) compatibility.

What AI checks in your CV

  • ATS keywords: does your CV contain the terms recruiters search for in this specific role?
  • Quantified impact: do your achievements include concrete numbers (%, €, team size, growth)?
  • Chronological consistency: are there unexplained gaps or date inconsistencies?
  • Market alignment: does your profile match the seniority level and salary band you are targeting?

02 — Strategy

5 Smarter Job Search Strategies for 2026

The modern job search has fundamentally changed. Blindly applying to dozens of listings without a strategy delivers the fewest results.

1. Tailor your CV to every job description (with AI help)

A generic CV rarely passes ATS filters. Use an automated CV analysis tool to identify missing keywords for each specific role before applying.

2. Define your market positioning before you start applying

An AI Career Path cross-references your profile against real market data and tells you exactly where you have the highest probability of success.

3. Optimise your LinkedIn before switching on "Open to Work"

LinkedIn is the first place recruiters will look. Make sure your headline, summary and experience sections are optimised with the right keywords for your target roles.

4. Quantify everything on your resume

"Responsible for the sales team" says nothing. "Led a team of 8, increasing revenue by 34% over 12 months" is compelling. AI immediately flags the absence of metrics.

5. Use market data to negotiate your salary

Before any interview, research real salary ranges for the role and sector. Entering a negotiation without data is the fastest way to leave money on the table.

03 — Common Mistakes

7 CV Mistakes AI Detects in Seconds (and Recruiters See Them Too)

Automated CV analysis identifies problems objectively — without the emotional filter that often prevents us from seeing our own flaws:

  • Generic professional objective — "Motivated professional seeking new challenges" adds zero value.
  • Absence of metrics — responsibilities without concrete results do not impress.
  • Missing ATS keywords — your CV is not aligned with the terms in job descriptions.
  • ATS-incompatible format — tables, columns and images confuse automated screening systems.
  • Unprofessional email — still using a 2008 Gmail with your nickname and date of birth?
  • Inconsistent dates — unexplained gaps raise immediate red flags.
  • CV too long — for most roles, 1 to 2 pages is the maximum.
