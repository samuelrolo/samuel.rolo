How to Improve Your LinkedIn to Appear
in Recruiter Searches

Samuel Rolo March 16, 2026 9 min read

LinkedIn has become one of the main platforms used by recruiters to identify candidates. But many professionals don't understand how the search logic works within the platform.

Small profile adjustments can significantly increase visibility. Here are the 5 most effective strategies.

1

A clear headline

The headline is one of the first things that appears in search results. It should include your area of expertise, relevant keywords and your professional value proposition. Avoid using only your current job title.

2

A well-structured "About" section

This section should tell your professional story clearly. Ideally it should include your experience, areas of expertise and professional goals. It's your space to create a coherent narrative.

3

Keywords in your experience

The search algorithms analyse professional experience descriptions. Including terms relevant to your field increases the likelihood of appearing in specific recruiter searches.

4

Relevant skills

The skills list helps recruiters quickly understand your professional positioning. Add the most relevant skills for your field and ask colleagues and former managers for endorsements.

5

Activity on the platform

Profiles that regularly share content or interact tend to have greater visibility in LinkedIn's algorithm. It's not necessary to post every day — consistency is more important than frequency.

The first step

Before optimising LinkedIn, it's useful to ensure that your CV conveys a clear professional narrative. Both documents should tell the same story in a coherent and complementary way.
