How to get a job in less than 90 days

Samuel Rolo 2 April 2026 8 min read

Most people search for a job without a method: they send applications without optimising their CV, ignore LinkedIn, don't track responses and miss opportunities due to lack of preparation. 90 days is more than enough time to change jobs — if you know what to do at each stage.

There is no magic formula for finding a job. But there is a method. And the difference between someone who takes 6 months and someone who lands a job in 90 days is rarely talent — it's strategy, consistency and the right tools.

This article shows you exactly what to do at each stage of the process, integrating Share2Inspire tools to maximise your time and your chances of success.

"Those who find a job faster aren't the ones who send the most applications — they're the ones who send the right applications, with the right positioning, at the right time."

1
Days 1 – 30

Diagnosis and positioning

Before sending a single application, you need to know exactly where you stand. An outdated CV, a LinkedIn profile invisible to recruiters or a poor reading of the market are the main reasons why applications go unanswered.

This phase has a clear objective: build a solid foundation. Optimised CV, fine-tuned LinkedIn profile and clarity about the market you're going to target.

Expected result after 30 days: CV optimised for ATS and recruiters, LinkedIn profile with active visibility and a clear map of the market you'll explore in the coming weeks.

The CV Analyser identifies exactly what's preventing your CV from passing automatic screening. It's not a generic review — it's an analysis based on your sector, seniority level and target roles. The LinkedIn Roaster does the same for your profile: it evaluates what a recruiter sees (and what they ignore) and tells you how to fix it with precision.

Career Intelligence gives you a real competitive advantage. Knowing that a particular role is saturated, that an adjacent area has 3x more demand, or that certain technical skills differentiate you — that information is worth far more than another blind application.

2
Days 31 – 60

Strategy and application management

With your positioning defined, it's time to take action. This phase is about creating an application system that doesn't rely on memory or spreadsheets — a real pipeline, with full visibility over every opportunity.

The most common mistake at this stage: applying without a method. Saved jobs in browser tabs, lost emails in the inbox, interviews scheduled without context. The result is predictable — lack of focus and missed opportunities.

3
Days 61 – 90

Execution, interviews and offer

Applications are progressing. You have interviews scheduled. Now the focus shifts: it's not about quantity — it's about quality of execution at every interaction. A good interview, a personalised cover letter, a networking email at the right moment — these details make the difference between an offer and silence.
