The 5-Phase Playbook

Phase 1

ATS-Friendly CV

Use the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result) with quantifiable metrics. Every bullet point must demonstrate measurable impact.

Phase 2

Digital Assessments

Practise Pymetrics (neuroscience games), Arctic Shores (behavioural), and SHL (numerical/verbal reasoning) before the real test.

Phase 3

HR Interview

Articulate intrinsic motivations for consulting — not salary or prestige. Show you understand the firm's culture and strategic direction.

Phase 4

Case Interview

Master the MECE framework (Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive) and practice mental math. Structure your thinking out loud.

Phase 5

Partner Interview

Demonstrate professional maturity, cultural fit, and executive presence. This is as much about who you are as what you know.

Bonus

Reapplication Strategy

Rejection is not the end. Most Big 4 firms allow reapplication after 6–12 months. Use the time to strengthen your profile systematically.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the acceptance rate at Big 4 firms?
The acceptance rate at Big 4 consulting firms (Deloitte, PwC, EY, KPMG) is only between 2% and 6%. Most candidates fail not due to lack of intelligence, but due to lack of preparation and understanding of the recruitment process.
What is the STAR method for a Big 4 CV?
STAR stands for Situation, Task, Action, Result. Each bullet point in your CV should follow this structure and include quantifiable metrics (e.g., "Reduced process time by 30%"). This format is optimised for ATS systems and human reviewers at consulting firms.
What are the digital assessments used by Big 4 firms?
Big 4 firms typically use Pymetrics (neuroscience-based games), Arctic Shores (behavioural assessments), and SHL (numerical and verbal reasoning tests). Practising these formats in advance significantly improves your performance.
What is the MECE framework for case interviews?
MECE stands for Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive. It is the core problem-solving framework used in consulting case interviews. Your analysis should cover all relevant areas without overlap, demonstrating structured thinking.
Can I reapply to a Big 4 firm after rejection?
Yes. Most Big 4 firms allow reapplication after a cool-down period (typically 6–12 months). Use the time to strengthen your profile, practise case interviews, and address the specific gaps from your previous application.
