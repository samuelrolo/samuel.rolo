7 Common CV Errors That Get Candidates Rejected

Read full article →

Your CV is often your first and only chance to make an impression. Yet many candidates make critical mistakes that lead to instant rejection.

Error 1: Poor formatting — Inconsistent fonts, spacing, or structure make your CV hard to read and process by ATS systems.

Error 2: Lack of keywords — Recruiters search for specific terms. If your CV doesn't match the job description, it won't be found.

Error 3: Vague descriptions — Generic statements don't demonstrate impact. Use metrics and results.

Error 4: Typos and grammar mistakes — These signal carelessness and lack of attention to detail.

Error 5: Outdated information — A CV that hasn't been updated in years raises questions about your engagement.

CV or LinkedIn: Which Is More Important for Your Career?

Read full article →

In recent years, LinkedIn has become almost as important as a traditional CV. The most honest answer is: both are essential, but they serve different functions.

The role of CV — The CV is the main document in formal application processes. It allows quick assessment of experience, progression and skills.

The role of LinkedIn — It functions as an extension of professional identity. Recruiters use it to search for candidates, validate experiences and analyze activity.

The most common problem — Many have a good CV but outdated LinkedIn, or vice versa. Both should convey a coherent narrative.

The ideal balance — Optimized CV for applications + LinkedIn prepared for recruiter searches = maximum opportunities.

Algorithmic Literacy

Essential Guide: ATS as the Digital Guardian of Your Career

"Your CV is no longer just a career narrative. In the digital ecosystem, it's a technical object — a set of data that needs to be structured to be successfully interpreted by extraction algorithms."

Reading time: 12 min

In today's job market, the recruitment process begins long before the interview. Before a human recruiter even sees your CV, it passes through an automated system — the ATS (Applicant Tracking System) — which functions as a digital gatekeeper.

The ATS analyzes your CV looking for specific keywords, compatible formatting and logical structure. If your CV isn't optimized for these systems, it will be automatically rejected, regardless of your experience or qualifications.

The 5 pillars of an ATS-compatible CV:

1. Clean formatting — No tables, columns or graphics that confuse the parser.
2. Strategic keywords — Terms extracted directly from the job description.
3. Standard sections — Experience, Education, Skills clearly identified.
4. File format — PDF or DOCX, never images or proprietary formats.
5. Temporal consistency — Clear dates, no unexplained gaps.

The reality is that 75% of CVs are rejected by ATS before reaching human hands. Understanding and mastering this technology is not optional — it's an essential employability skill for the 21st century.

Employability 2026

The CV Is Not What It Seems: 5 Lessons on Algorithmic Employability

"Your CV is no longer read first by people. It's processed. Modern employability requires the shift from perception to actionable data."

Reading time: 10 min

The paradigm of employability has changed radically. The CV, once a narrative document designed to impress a human reader, has transformed into a technical object that needs to be interpreted by algorithms.

Lesson 1: Your CV is a dataset, not a story.
Modern recruitment systems extract structured data from your CV. Each word, each date, each skill is converted into fields of a database. If information isn't clear and structured, it's lost in translation.

Lesson 2: Keywords are not buzzwords.
There's a fundamental difference between filling your CV with jargon and strategically using the terms recruiters and algorithms search for. The key is aligning your CV language with market language.

Lesson 3: Consistency across platforms is measured.
Recruiters cross-reference your CV with LinkedIn. Inconsistencies in dates, titles or skills raise immediate red flags.

Lesson 4: Format matters as much as content.
A visually stunning CV that doesn't pass through ATS is an invisible CV. Form must serve function.

Lesson 5: Employability is a skill, not a state.
Staying employable requires constant updating — of your CV, LinkedIn, skills and market awareness.
